01 — About

A little about me

I'm Souvik, a 20-year-old from Siliguri, currently studying Physics at Surya Sen Mahavidyalaya. I am drawn to the deep, quiet questions — the ones that sit at the edge of what we understand about light, matter, and life.

My particular curiosity leans toward photonics and particle physics — fields where the boundary between the observable and the theoretical feels most alive. There is something humbling about studying a universe that consistently exceeds human imagination.

Outside the classroom, I have spent nearly ten years caring for animals. Over the years I've shared my home with rabbits, cats, birds, turtles, rats, and fish. Today, two budgerigars chirp through my mornings, a community aquarium tank brings calm to my desk, and a betta fish bowl holds its own small, vivid world.

Music has been another constant thread. I play the mandolin, tabla, harmonica, and Hawaiian guitar — each one a different texture of thought. Some days I code instead: Python for scientific computation, Java, and C++. I'm currently learning web development, finding it unexpectedly meditative.

This page is a small, unhurried corner of the internet — a place to share what I'm thinking about, making, and caring for.

05 — Creating

Aquatic Stories

Beyond keeping aquariums, I find myself reaching for my phone to document them. There's something worth preserving in how light refracts through water, how a betta trails its fins through plants, or how a school of serpae tetras and zebra danios moves as one.

I run a small Instagram page — @aquavik21 — where I post short reels around my tanks, aquatic life, and the quiet, meditative aesthetic of a well-kept ecosystem. It's less about follower counts and more about having a place where these little worlds get their moment.

The content is unhurried, unscripted, and usually filmed during the same late evenings I spend feeding the fish.
